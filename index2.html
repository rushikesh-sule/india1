Top Destinations

Taj Mahal, Agra

One of the Seven Wonders of the World, a symbol of love and Mughal architecture.

Kerala Backwaters

Enjoy a peaceful boat ride through the lush and green waterways of God's Own Country.

Leh-Ladakh

High mountains, beautiful monasteries, and thrilling adventure routes await.

Hawa Mahal, Jaipur

The iconic "Palace of Winds" known for its stunning pink sandstone architecture and cultural heritage of Rajasthan.

Ghats of Varanasi

One of the world's oldest living cities, known for its holy Ganga Aarti, spirituality, and ancient traditions.

Sikkim

A paradise in the Northeast with snow-capped peaks, monasteries, and the beautiful Gurudongmar Lake.

Meenakshi Temple, Madurai

An architectural marvel in Tamil Nadu dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi, famous for its tall gopurams and sculptures.

Andaman Islands

Crystal-clear beaches, coral reefs, and water sports make Andaman a dream destination for beach lovers.

Major Festivals of India

Diwali

Festival of Lights celebrated with diyas, crackers, sweets, and joy, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness.

Holi

The festival of colors, celebrated with vibrant powders, music, dance, and delicious sweets like gujiya.

Eid-ul-Fitr

Marks the end of Ramadan, celebrated with prayers, feasts, and giving charity to the poor.

Christmas

Celebrated across India with carols, church prayers, and decorations marking the birth of Jesus Christ.

Onam

A harvest festival of Kerala, featuring boat races, floral decorations, and the grand Onam Sadya feast.

Dussehra

Celebrates the victory of good over evil, with the burning of Ravan effigies and grand Ramleela performances.

Ganesh Chaturthi

Festival of Lord Ganesha, celebrated with vibrant processions and idol immersions.

Navratri

Dedicated to Goddess Durga, celebrated with 9 nights of garba dance, music, and fasting.

India is known for its vibrant festivals, each filled with colors, rituals, music, and joy across all religions and regions.
